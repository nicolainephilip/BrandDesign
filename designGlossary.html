  • Design Terms
  • Brand Identity
  • If you’re working with me or using any of my design products, you might come across a few terms that feel unfamiliar. This glossary will help you understand the basics so you can feel confident about what you're getting.
  • Logo
  • A symbol or text that represents your business. It can be a wordmark (text only), icon, or a combination.
  • Mockup
  • A realistic preview of how your design will look in real life — like your logo on a tote bag or business card.
  • Typography
  • The style and arrangement of text. This includes fonts, sizing, spacing, and how text is laid out.
  • Palette / Color Scheme
  • The set of colors used in your brand. These are chosen to reflect your mood, message, and target audience.
  • Layout
  • How elements (like text, images, or logos) are arranged on a design, such as a flyer or business card.
  • Vector File (AI, SVG, EPS)
  • A scalable file format that can be resized without losing quality. Perfect for printing at any size.
  • Raster File (PNG, JPG)
  • A pixel-based file. Great for web or small print use, but can lose quality if stretched too large.
  • Bleed
  • Extra space around the design that gets trimmed after printing. Important for designs like flyers or packaging.
  • Margin / Safe Zone
  • The area within your design where important content should stay so it doesn’t get cut off in printing.
  • CMYK / RGB
  • CMYK is used for print. RGB is for screens. Designs should be in the correct mode depending on where they'll be used.
  • DPI / Resolution
  • Stands for "dots per inch." Higher DPI = better print quality. 300 DPI is standard for print.
  • Style Guide / Brand Guidelines
  • A document that shows how your brand should look across all platforms — includes logo rules, fonts, colors, and more.
